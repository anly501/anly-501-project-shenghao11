Conclusion

My topic is about the natural population growth rate of each country in the world. I collected data on economic crime, unemployment rates, etc. for different countries and compared it to the population growth rate to determine what factors are causing the natural population growth/decline and to predict future population changes for each country.

After collecting the data, I cleaned the data and used machine learning algorithms such as naive bayes, decision trees, SVM, clustering, ARM machine learning methods to analysis the data.

To summarize all the algorithms, GDP, economy, and life expectancy seem to have some effect on the natural birth rate. Population growth is generally in developing countries, and population decline is generally in developed countries. In any developed country, the higher the human population, or the educational level of the social class and the GDP per capita, the fewer children are born. In countries, there is usually an inverse relationship between income and total fertility. As life expectancy increases, the mean age at childbearing is a measure of declining fertility. On the other hand, economic growth occurs at the beginning of high fertility; as economic growth accelerates, fertility declines.

Future directions for research, how the natural rate of population growth in turn affects the economy, disparities, life expectancy, future population projections for each country, and whether it is possible to control the rate of population growth.
